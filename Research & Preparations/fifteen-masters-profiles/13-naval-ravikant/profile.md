## 3. Core Philosophy

### The Naval Framework

**1. Seek Wealth, Not Money or Status:**
- Wealth = Assets that earn while you sleep
- Money = Transferred wealth (payment for time)
- Status = Your place in social hierarchy

**2. Make Abundance for the World:**
- Create value, capture portion
- Don't extract from others
- Positive-sum thinking

**3. Free Markets Are Intrinsic to Humans:**
- We are the only species that trades
- Markets enable specialization
- Wealth creation is not zero-sum

**4. Specific Knowledge is Key:**
- Cannot be taught in school
- Found by pursuing genuine curiosity
- Often technical and creative
- Cannot be automated easily

**5. Accountability Builds Credibility:**
- Put skin in the game
- Take risks under your own name
- Reputation compounds

**6. Leverage Allows Scale:**
- **Labor**: Oldest form (people working for you)
- **Capital**: Money working for you
- **Code/Media**: Zero marginal cost replication

**7. Judgment Requires Experience:**
- Decision-making is the skill
- Learn from outcomes
- Pattern recognition

**8. Play Long-Term Games:**
- Compound interest in relationships
- Trust and reputation build over time
- Short-term thinking destroys value

### Specific Knowledge

**What is Specific Knowledge?**
> "Specific knowledge is found much more by pursuing your innate talents, your genuine curiosity, and your passion."

**Characteristics:**
- Cannot be taught (only learned)
- Unique to the individual
- Often feels like play to you, work to others
- Technical AND creative
- Cannot be easily outsourced or automated

**Examples:**
- Sales ability
- Artistic talent
- Deep technical expertise
- Unique perspective
- Network effects

### Happiness Philosophy

**The Path:**
> "Desire is a contract you make with yourself to be unhappy until you get what you want."

**Key Principles:**
1. **Health first** — Body and mind
2. **No expectations** — Don't suffer imagined troubles
3. **Present moment** — Now is all there is
4. **Freedom** — Time and location freedom
5. **Relationships** — Quality over quantity

**The Three Things:**
1. **Wealth** — Freedom from financial worry
2. **Health** — Physical and mental vitality
3. **Happiness** — Peace of mind

### Reading Philosophy

**Naval on Reading:**
> "Read what you love until you love to read."

**Approach:**
- Don't finish books you don't enjoy
- Read broadly across disciplines
- Old books > new books (Lindy effect)
- Fiction and non-fiction
- No book is mandatory

## 5. Problem-Solving Methodology

### The Naval Approach

**1. First Principles Thinking:**
- Break to fundamentals
- Question assumptions
- Build from there

**2. Long-Term Optimization:**
- Think in decades
- Compound relationships
- Build reputation

**3. Specific Knowledge:**
- Find what you're uniquely good at
- It should feel like play
- Double down on strengths

**4. Leverage:**
- Code (zero marginal cost)
- Media (content creation)
- Capital (investing)
- Labor (team building)

**5. Permissionless:**
- Don't wait for approval
- Build in public
- Let results speak

### Case Study: AngelList Creation

**Problem:** Startups needed funding, angels needed deal flow

**Naval's Approach:**
1. Started simple email list
2. Built community around it
3. Added tools (syndicates, rolling funds)
4. Democratized access
5. Created new asset class

**Result:** 1000+ startups funded, new investing model

### Case Study: Personal Brand

**Problem:** How to share wisdom at scale?

**Naval's Approach:**
1. Tweet pithy insights
2. Answer questions thoughtfully
3. Podcast for deeper dives
4. Let others compile (Almanack)
5. No marketing, just value

**Result:** Millions follow, influence without seeking it
