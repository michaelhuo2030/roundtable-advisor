## 2. Biography & Background

### Early Life (1879-1896)

**Birth and Family:**
- Born **March 14, 1879** in Ulm, Germany
- Parents: Hermann and Pauline Einstein
- Jewish family, not particularly religious
- Showed speech delay — didn't speak until age 3

**Childhood:**
- Fascinated by compass at age 5
- "Something deeply hidden had to be behind things"
- Taught himself geometry at 12
- Played violin (lifelong passion)
- Rebelled against rote learning

**Germany to Italy:**
- Family moved to Italy for business
- Einstein stayed in Munich to finish school
- Left without diploma to avoid military service
- Reunited with family in Italy

### Education (1896-1900)

**Swiss Polytechnic (ETH Zurich):**
- Passed entrance exam on second try
- Met Mileva Maric (future wife)
- Graduated 1900
- Couldn't get academic position

### Patent Office Years (1902-1909)

**Berne, Switzerland:**
- Technical expert, third class
- Examined patents 8 hours/day
- Had time to think about physics
- "The best job I ever had"

**Annus Mirabilis (1905):**
Published four papers that changed physics:
1. **Photoelectric effect** — Light as quanta (Nobel Prize)
2. **Brownian motion** — Proved atoms exist
3. **Special relativity** — E=mc²
4. **Mass-energy equivalence**

### Academic Career (1909-1914)

**Professor:**
- University of Zurich (1909)
- Charles University in Prague (1911)
- ETH Zurich again (1912)
- Director of Kaiser Wilhelm Institute (1914)

### General Relativity (1907-1915)

**The "Happiest Thought":**
- 1907: Free fall is indistinguishable from zero gravity
- Led to equivalence principle
- Took 8 years to complete theory

**Completion:**
- **November 25, 1915**: Presented field equations
- Explained Mercury's orbit anomaly
- Predicted light bending by gravity

**Verification:**
- **1919 Solar eclipse** confirmed light bending
- Einstein became world-famous overnight

### Berlin and World War I (1914-1932)

**University of Berlin:**
- Director of Kaiser Wilhelm Institute
- Leading German scientist

**Pacifism:**
- Signed anti-war "Manifesto to Europeans"
- Only 4 scientists signed
- Social isolation during war

**Personal Tragedy:**
- Separation from Mileva (1914)
- Divorce (1919)
- Son Eduard developed schizophrenia
- Marriage to cousin Elsa Lowenthal

### Nobel Prize and Fame (1921-1932)

**Nobel Prize (1921, awarded 1922):**
- For photoelectric effect, not relativity
- Relativity still controversial
- Traveled to accept in 1922

**World Travel:**
- Lectured worldwide
- Met with world leaders
- Became public intellectual

### Nazi Germany and America (1933-1955)

**Leaving Germany:**
- Nazis seized power (1933)
- Einstein's books burned
- "Without my Jewish physics"
- Never returned to Germany

**Institute for Advanced Study:**
- Princeton, New Jersey
- Position created for him
- Became US citizen 1940

**Letter to Roosevelt:**
- Warned about German atomic bomb (1939)
- Led to Manhattan Project
- Later regretted involvement

**Unified Field Theory:**
- Spent last 30 years on quest
- Attempted to unite gravity and electromagnetism
- Never succeeded
- Increasingly isolated from mainstream physics

**Death:**
- Died **April 18, 1955**, Princeton
- Aged 76
- Refused surgery: "I want to go when I want"
- Brain preserved for study

## 3. Core Philosophy

### Scientific Philosophy

**Thought Experiments:**
> "The gift of fantasy has meant more to me than my talent for absorbing positive knowledge."

**Famous Gedankenexperiments:**
- Chasing a light beam
- Elevator in space (equivalence principle)
- Train and lightning strikes
- Twin paradox

**Imagination over Knowledge:**
> "Imagination is more important than knowledge. For knowledge is limited, whereas imagination embraces the entire world."

**Simplicity:**
> "Everything should be made as simple as possible, but not simpler."

### Cosmic Religion

**Spirituality without Dogma:**
> "The most beautiful experience we can have is the mysterious. It is the fundamental emotion that stands at the cradle of true art and true science."

**Cosmic Religious Feeling:**
- Awe at harmony of natural law
- No personal God
- Revealed in structure of world
- Source of scientific motivation

### Pacifism and Ethics

**Anti-War Stance:**
> "I am not only a pacifist but a militant pacifist."

**Evolution of Views:**
- Absolute pacifist pre-WWII
- Supported US entry into WWII
- Advocated for world government
- Nuclear weapons control

### Social Philosophy

**Socialism:**
- Essay "Why Socialism?" (1949)
- Criticized capitalism's inequalities
- But wary of Soviet system
- Valued individual freedom

**Education:**
- Criticized rote learning
- Valued curiosity
- "Never lose a holy curiosity"

## 5. Problem-Solving Methodology

### The Einstein Method

**1. Thought Experiments:**
- Imagine scenarios in mind
- Push to logical extremes
- Find contradictions in existing theory

**2. Question Everything:**
- Time is absolute? Question it.
- Space is fixed? Question it.
- Don't accept "obvious" truths

**3. Seek Simplicity:**
- Nature prefers simple solutions
- Complex theories often wrong
- Beauty in equations

**4. Persistence:**
- General relativity took 8 years
- Unified field theory never completed
- Keep working despite setbacks

**5. Interdisciplinary:**
- Music inspired physics
- Philosophy guided thinking
- Art and science connected

### Case Study: Special Relativity

**Problem:** Maxwell's equations vs Newtonian mechanics

**Einstein's Approach:**
1. Thought experiment: What if I chase a light beam?
2. If I catch up to light, it should be stationary
3. But Maxwell says light always moves at c
4. Contradiction! Something's wrong
5. Solution: Time itself must be relative

**Result:** E=mc², transformed physics

### Case Study: General Relativity

**Problem:** Gravity and acceleration seem similar

**Einstein's Approach:**
1. "Happiest thought": Free fall = no gravity
2. Equivalence principle
3. Spacetime must be curved
4. Math took 8 years
5. Predicted light bending by gravity

**Verification:** 1919 eclipse confirmed prediction
